-= Troubles with Electric Transport =-

I've been watching videos about electric transport and have identified these main hurdles:

  1. Laws against light electric vehicles. Limiting scooter speeds, capping bicycle power, forcing insurance purchases - each country comes up with its own regulations, but they all tend to tighten controls and make electric transport more complex and expensive.
  2. Electricity prices: It's only really practical to charge electric vehicles at home. Scooters are advantageous in this regard, whereas electric cars would sit at charging stations paying a threefold premium for electricity, not to mention additional fees for time spent charging.
  3. Lack of charging stations. This is the same problem from another angle. Electricity is readily available in most homes, but charging stations are scarce. For instance, if I left my car charging at a station, now I'd have to walk several kilometers back home? And scooters would also benefit from charging stations - there are even fewer of them, or none at all.
